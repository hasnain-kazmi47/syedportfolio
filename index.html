Electric Fleets increasing GDP

The impact of UK financial incentives on the adoption of electric fleets

This article explains on how the adoption of electric fleets (EF) in the UK has the connection to changes in GDP, financial buying incentives, and EF adoption. The article shows results which state that the financial purchase incentives have a favorable impact on adoptions of EF. Further three categories of adopters were identified Based on the decision to adopt, we identify three different categories of EF adopters First is insensitive adopters who adopt EFs when financial purchase incentives and GDP change are below their average levels. Second are Standard adopters who adopt when GDP change and financial buy incentives are close to average levels and the third are sensitive adopters who adopt when GDP change and financial purchase incentives are both above average levels. Overall, 98.48% of enterprises fall into this last category of sensitive EF adopters.

UK Economy after COVID

How has the UK economy fared in the two tumultuous years since Covid?

The initial Covid-19 wave and Britain's tardy entry into a tight lockdown triggered a sudden pause in activity across the nation resulting in the country's worst recession in a century. In the second quarter of 2020 and for the entire year the UK's GDP fell by over 20%. In the wake of COVID-19, inflation has reached its worst level since 1992, and Russia's invasion of Ukraine threatens to make matters worse. The cost of life has increased dramatically throughout the world because of disruptions to global supply lines, a lack of employees and supplies, and an increase in demand following the lockdown. Brexit has made trade disruptions in the UK worse. In 2020–21, the UK government's budget deficit—the difference between spending and income—rose to a record-high level for a peacetime year, reaching about £318 billion, as tax revenues plummeted and the government poured billions of dollars into emergency pandemic aid, including furloughs, business grants, and loans.
